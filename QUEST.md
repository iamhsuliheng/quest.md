# QUEST.md

A semantic specification for project documents.

## What is a quest?

Any undertaking with a name and an imaginable completion state.

The completion state does not need to be measurable. It needs to be
imaginable: you can picture the scene — what exists, who is there,
what has changed — when the quest is done.

## What is a QUEST.md?

A record of two imaginations and the reality between them.

**Destination** is your imagination of where you are going.
**Route** is your imagination of how you will get there.
**Current Situation** is the reality you are navigating through.

Both imaginations are held under pressure from reality. They are
expected to change. The Journal preserves that history.

## When to create a QUEST.md

Create a QUEST.md when an undertaking meets either condition:

- It requires more than one step to complete, or
- It cannot be finished in a single sitting.

## Structure

A QUEST.md has two layers.

### Strategic layer

The strategic layer answers: *why does this quest exist, and what
are we choosing to do about it?* Establish this before the tactical
layer.

#### Crisis

The world before the journey began, and the event that broke it.
Describe what was normal, what happened to disturb that normal, and
why continuing as before is no longer possible.

#### Resolution

The chosen direction in response to the Crisis. Not the destination
and not the route — the strategic orientation that points toward
both. Describe what kind of response this quest represents and why
this direction over others.

### Tactical layer

The tactical layer answers: *where are we going, where are we now,
how will we get there, and who is with us?*

#### Destination

The imaginable scene of completion. Describe the moment you will
know you have arrived — a scene, a state, or an image concrete
enough that everyone working toward it can recognize it when it
happens. Include why this destination is worth going to.

#### Current Situation

Where you are right now in relation to the Destination. Describe
the present state and the gap between here and there. The most
important sentence in this section names what is blocking forward
movement and why.

#### Route

The imagined journey toward the Destination — what the path looks
like, what actions it involves, and why this route over others.
Include the constraints that shape this path: what resources are
available, what approaches are off the table, and what conditions
are fixed.

Like Destination, Route is an imagination held under pressure from
reality. The Journal records how it changes.

#### Fellowship

Who is on this journey and what they bring. Include who is already
involved, what resources and capabilities are available, and who or
what is still needed. Gathering the Fellowship is often the first
step on the Route.

#### Journal

A record of how the quest has moved. Each entry notes a change in
any of the sections above — what changed and why.

Format: `YYYY-MM-DD — what changed and why`

## Sentence types

Every sentence in a QUEST.md can be identified by its function
within its section, using the 5W1H framework:

- **Who** — the people or roles involved
- **What** — the state, action, or object being described
- **When** — timing, deadlines, or duration
- **Where** — the domain, context, or location
- **Why** — the reason, motivation, or cause
- **How** — the method, constraint, or means

Not every section needs to cover all six. The framework is a
reading tool, not a checklist.

## Design principles

**Imaginable over measurable.** The test for a good Destination is
not whether it can be quantified, but whether the people working
toward it can picture it clearly enough to recognize when they have
arrived.

**Semantic over structural.** QUEST.md defines what sentences mean,
not how documents look. Prose, lists, and tables are all valid. A
document with only a Crisis and a Destination is a valid QUEST.md.

**Living over static.** A QUEST.md is not a plan written once. It
is a record of two imaginations navigating reality. All sections
are expected to change. The Journal preserves that history.

**Strategy before tactics.** Establish Crisis and Resolution before
developing Destination and Route. Tactical clarity built on an
unexamined Crisis tends to drift.
