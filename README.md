# QUEST.md

A semantic specification for project documents.

Any project — writing a book, organizing an event, launching a
product, running a campaign — can be described as a quest: you are
trying to get somewhere, you are somewhere right now, and you have
a path between the two.

QUEST.md gives that structure a name.

## The structure

A QUEST.md has two layers and a running record.

**Strategic layer** — why this quest exists
- `Crisis` — the event that made this quest necessary
- `Resolution` — the chosen direction in response, and why not the
  obvious alternatives

**Tactical layer** — how the quest unfolds
- `Destination` — the imaginable scene of completion
- `Current Situation` — where you are right now
- `Route` — the imagined path, and its constraints
- `Fellowship` — who is on this journey

**Journal** — a running record of how things change
- `Takeaways` — what the quest has taught you so far; updated as
  understanding grows
- `Entries` — a chronological log of what changed and why

## The core idea

A QUEST.md holds two imaginations and the reality between them.

**Destination** is your imagination of where you are going.
**Route** is your imagination of how you will get there.
**Current Situation** is the reality you are navigating through.

Both imaginations are expected to change under pressure from
reality. The Journal records that history.

## Getting started

Create a `QUEST.md` in your project. Fill in the sections in this
order:

1. **Crisis** — what broke the normal state and made this quest
   necessary
2. **Resolution** — the direction you are choosing in response, and
   why not the most obvious alternatives
3. **Destination** — the scene you are trying to reach
4. **Current Situation** — where you are right now and what is
   blocking you
5. **Route** — how you plan to get there, and the constraints
   shaping that path
6. **Fellowship** — who is involved and what is still needed

Start the **Journal** when something first changes. Add an entry
each time a section shifts. Update **Takeaways** when you have
learned something worth distilling.

Add `Status: active` at the top of the document. Update it to
`completed` when the Destination is reached, or `abandoned` if the
quest ends before that.

Fill in what you know. Leave blank what you don't. A document with
only a Crisis and a Destination is already a valid QUEST.md.
